Pencil Sketches

Our design process was one with many iterations as it should be! I started off taking the key features identified in the user flows, and very roughly sketched these out, as shown below. This step was focused on functionality and making sure that all the elements were there, with no focus on copy or visuals at this point.

Mid-fidelity Wireframes

From there, I designed some mid-fidelity wireframes. After some feedback on these, I upgraded them to high fidelity wireframes. At this point, I needed to start thinking much more about the copy and positioning of elements as I needed to start getting some feedback on the usability of the design.

High-fidelity Wireframes

Once I had gotten some feedback on these wireframes, I took it another step further and started designing high fidelity prototypes. I knew that I would be using these to conduct user testing, so they needed to be a really strong balance of clear enough design to communicate with the user, but not spend too much time on the details of the visuals.

Prototype
